Katse Dam

Katse Dam, located in the Mohales Hoek District of Lesotho, is one of the most spectacular places to visit in the country. Situated between two mountain ranges, the dam is a stunning sight to behold. It is the second largest dam in Africa and provides the perfect backdrop for a variety of activities. Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, there’s something for everyone at Katse Dam. Here are 10 things to do when you visit this incredible location.

Maletsunyane waterfall

The waterfall is on the Maletsunyane River and it falls from a ledge of Triassic-Jurassic basalt. The plunging water creates a reverberating echo when it contact the basin of the falls, and local legend has it that the sound comes from the wailing of people who have drowned in the falls.

Liphoofung Cave

The Liphoofung caves offer a uniqye glimpse into the regions history and culture, making them a must visit destination for anyone exploring the stunning landscape of Lesotho.Whether you'are an archaeology enthusiast, a nature lover , or simply seeking adventure,the caves provide a rich experience that connects visitors to the ancient past.
